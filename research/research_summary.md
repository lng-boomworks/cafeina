## 1. Current State Summary

Cafeina (also styled "Caféina") is a family-run vintage café in San Fulgencio (Alicante), Spain, trading since 2009. The existing site positions the business as a "Relaxing Vintage Café" serving coffee, tea, cakes, and craft cocktails, with explicit British afternoon-tea framing aimed at the mixed local + expat / tourist audience in La Marina. The current owners are Callum and Millie, who took over from the original founder Maria; the site leans heavily on that family-handoff narrative.

- **Existing website**: https://cafeinalamarina.com/
- **Social channels covered**: Facebook page exists (`facebook.com/cafeinavintage`). The homepage shows a Facebook icon but it isn't linked, suggesting the social presence is under-maintained.
- **Google Business Profile**: Not confirmed during audit — worth checking as it's likely the strongest existing review source.
- **Overall digital maturity**: **LOW–MEDIUM** — site exists and has a decent structure, but content is thin, menus are placeholder, trust signals are minimal, and imagery is stock.

### Observations

- **Information architecture is reasonable** but execution is incomplete: `Home → Our Menus (5 submenus) → Our Story → Events → Contact Us`.
- **Brand tone is clear and likeable**: warm, personal, "with love / since 2009", uses phrases like "cozy nook" and frames the team handoff sincerely. This is a real asset to preserve.
- **Menus are effectively empty**: the Cocktail Collection page shows a single Pornstar Martini repeated three times; the Cake and Snacks page shows one item (Lemon Drizzle Cake, €3.50). Other menu routes exist but were not populated during the audit.
- **Events page is placeholder**: describes Wine Tasting and "Fiestas La Marina Style" generically with no dates; banner says "new events starting in May" with no specifics.
- **Trust is under-resourced**: a single homepage testimonial attributed to "Millicent Nicholson-Gates" is the only social proof anywhere — this reads as family rather than independent endorsement.
- **Functional issues**: phone `tel:` link on the homepage is truncated (`tel:6354779`); no Google Maps embed on the contact page; no schema markup; no newsletter capture; no booking system.
- **Language**: English-first with a Spanish toggle — consistent with the La Marina expat market.

### Weaknesses

- Menu pages carry no weight; a visitor can't browse what's actually available.
- Zero independent social proof (no reviews widget, no press, no ratings badge).
- Events are vague and undated; "new events in May" signals a stale page.
- Stock photography throughout (credited Pexels) — no brand identity in the imagery.
- Facebook icon that doesn't link; abandoned-feel social footer.
- No online booking or reservation — a real gap given the afternoon tea offering.
- Broken phone `tel:` link above the fold.
- No address pin / embedded map on the contact page.

### Opportunities

- Proper menu pages with categories, prices, and dietary tags.
- Real branded photography: interior, staff, signature items.
- Live events calendar with booking (wine tastings, fiestas, murder mystery, bottomless prosecco).
- Reviews widget (Google and/or Facebook) once feed access is confirmed.
- Simple online reservation — at minimum for afternoon tea.
- Map embed + directions on contact, with hours schema.
- Local-business structured data for SEO (opening hours, address, serves cuisine).
- WhatsApp CTA (common in Spain).
- Newsletter / loyalty signup that actually explains the tiers.

## 4. Opportunities to Outperform

- **Build real menu pages**: four or five categorised menus with items, prices, and brief copy — with dietary tags and "house speciality" markers.
  - _Grounded in_: current menus are placeholders (single cocktail / single cake).
- **Make the family-handoff story the emotional spine of the site**: Maria → Millie + Callum, "serving with love since 2009".
  - _Grounded in_: this is the most distinctive and authentic asset in the existing copy and it's currently buried on one page.
- **Treat afternoon tea as a hero offer, not a mention**: dedicated section on Home, bookable slot, imagery, pricing, sparkling upgrade.
  - _Grounded in_: it's the clearest British-in-Spain differentiator and currently gets one paragraph.
- **Put real reviews on the homepage** as soon as a feed source is confirmed (Google reviews widget is the low-friction default).
  - _Grounded in_: the only current testimonial is in-family; removing that and replacing with independent stars is an immediate credibility lift.
- **Events calendar with dates and a reservation form** instead of a "keep an eye out" paragraph.
  - _Grounded in_: current events page is placeholder and actively signals a stale site.
- **Branded photography** — interior, team, plated cakes, cocktails in-situ.
  - _Grounded in_: stock imagery dilutes the "cozy nook" positioning the copy is trying to build.

## 5. Content Direction

- **Tone of voice**: warm, personal, slightly indulgent; first-person-plural ("we"); uses affectionate phrasing ("with love", "cozy nook"); never corporate. Preserve the existing voice — it's working.
- **Reading level**: conversational, grade 6–7; short sentences; minimal jargon.
- **Core topics / service framing**: coffee + tea + cakes as the everyday offer; craft cocktails as the evening upgrade; afternoon tea as the signature experience; events as community rhythm.
- **Trust signals to prioritise**: "since 2009" heritage, family-run handoff, homemade cakes, Google/FB reviews (once wired), table-service (vs. counter).
- **Hero headline direction**: lead on the "vintage café + afternoon tea + cocktails" triad. Sub-headline leans on the "La Marina, since 2009" locator.
- **Primary CTA direction**: reservation-first ("Book a table" / "Reserve afternoon tea"), with a secondary "See the menu" for browsers.

## 6. Differentiation Strategy

**One-line differentiator**: The only vintage British-style café in La Marina serving proper afternoon tea and craft cocktails, family-run since 2009.

### Proof points

1. **Heritage**: trading since 2009, family handoff from Maria to Millie + Callum — not a new arrival.
2. **Offer breadth**: morning coffee through afternoon tea into evening cocktails — most cafés in the area don't bridge day-to-night.
3. **British-in-Spain specificity**: scones, clotted cream, sparkling afternoon tea — deliberate niche the local market is underserved on.

### Messaging architecture

- **Headline idea**: _Vintage café, La Marina — coffee, afternoon tea & cocktails since 2009._
- **Sub-headline idea**: _Family-run by Millie & Callum. Table service, homemade cakes, and a cozy nook in the heart of the community._
- **Three-word brand essence**: _Vintage · Warm · Local_
